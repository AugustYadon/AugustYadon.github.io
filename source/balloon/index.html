Jiggly Balloons


It's important in any art form to identify a focal point and spend extra time adding an appropriate amount of detail and extra work to it in order to bring life and personality to your project. The main difference I've noticed between AAA titles and indie games is the massive amount of detail that they add to objects that are not focal points, like pots or small bits of foliage just because they have the artists to do it. This is not necesary to make a game better, it's purpose is almost always to make a game "feel" more full and polished. That's not a bad thing at all, but if you're a small company or team, it's not efficient for you to add things to make the game "seem" more full before you have the resources to polish the focal points of the game. Please take this all as opinion and please contact me with your own opinions and experiences, I'd love to discuss all things "game development" with anyone who has something to share!

In VR, the focal points are usually objects you're able to pick up, or characters that come toward you. To make the player feel like there is something deeper that a flat lifeless 3D model, often you want to add animations, interaction scripts, and most importantly haptic feedback(controller vibration feedback usually). Seeing a sword clank against another sword with "clanking" sounds and physics is one thing, but to feel your sword grinding against a metal surface (or the increasing tension in your bowstring) is on another level. It's the layer of feedback that pushes you over the edge, where you allow yourself in a certain respect, to believe the ruse.

I'm not writing a book here, so I'll get on with the water balloon explanation/guide now that you know why this can be such an important piece of game. When you shake a balloon it should jiggle, it should deform from the water sinking on one side or the other based on gravity and how the player is tilting it, and it should pop is a satisfying way. We don't throw water balloons in real life so that we can get things wet. If we wanted to get things wet we'd use a bucket or a hose. Water balloons are just so satisfying to pop. Keep that in mind when you're desigining things based on 'satisfying feelings', what is it that is satisfying, what is suposed to be enjoyable about this thing?
